At a Glance: Core Differences

  • AS primarily uses share ownership and voting rights to determine control. Ind AS 110 and IFRS 10 use a unified 'Control Model' based on power and variable returns.
  • De facto control (control without majority voting rights) requires consolidation under IFRS/Ind AS, a concept generally absent in old AS.
  • Uniform accounting policies across the group are strictly required for consolidation under Ind AS/IFRS, whereas AS allowed exceptions if impracticable.

4.7 Special Situations (Ind AS 110)

1
SPEs (Banks/Real Estate): Must consolidate Securitization SPVs if risk retained. Toll roads/power SPVs consolidated if sponsor controls. Off-BS loans return to BS.
2
De Facto Control (VCs/Startups): VCs forced to consolidate portfolio companies if they exercise substantive control (board seats, operations) despite minority stakes.
3
Investment Entities (PE Funds): PE/VC funds are exempt from consolidation. They measure portfolio at Fair Value through P&L (Ind AS 109).
